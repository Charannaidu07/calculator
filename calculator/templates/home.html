Facts On Calculator

Historical Evolution: The earliest calculators were mechanical devices, such as the abacus, which dates back to ancient times. The first mechanical calculator, the Pascaline, was invented by Blaise Pascal in 1642.

Electronic Revolution: The first electronic calculator, the ANITA (A New Inspiration To Arithmetic), was developed in 1961. It was one of the earliest devices to use transistors instead of vacuum tubes.

Pocket Calculators: In the 1970s, pocket calculators became popular due to the development of integrated circuits, making them affordable and portable. The first widely used pocket calculator was the HP-35, introduced by Hewlett-Packard in 1972.

Graphing Calculators: The first graphing calculator, the Casio fx-7000G, was released in 1985. It allowed users to plot graphs, solve equations, and perform more complex calculations than traditional calculators.
